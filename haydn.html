HAYDN

(1732-1809)

Franz Joseph Haydn was a composer from Austria. He was considered to be instrumental in the development of Chamber music such as string quartets and the piano trio. The majority of his musical career was spent playing for wealthy family. This isolated him from other musicians and forced him to take on a more original approach to composition.

Famous Works

  • Symphony No. 94
  • Trumpet Concerto
  • Cello Concerto No. 1
  • Piano Sonata Hob. XVI/20
  • Symphony No. 45
