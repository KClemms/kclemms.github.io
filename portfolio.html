CLASSICAL ERA

(1750-1830)

The term classical music (lowercase c) is used to define western instrumental music, but when Classical is spelled with a capital C, it is defining a period of music that is sometimes referred to as Viennese Classicism. During this period, classical music broadened to include more types of music. At this time, the harpsichord was replaced with the piano. Also, the piano lost its position as the musical foundation of the orchestra. The sonatas and symphonies became more popular as well as string quartets.
