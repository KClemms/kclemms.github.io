WILLIAM BYRD

(1540-1623)

William Byrd is considered to be one of the greatest composers of music during the Renaissance period. He composed in sacred and secular polyphony (music consisting of 2 or more lines of music playing different melodies), as well as keyboard and consort music. Later in his career, he began writing sacred music for the Catholic church.

His Most Famous Works

  • Psalms, Sonnets and Songs of Sadness and Pietie (1588)
  • Mass for 4 Voices, the King's singers
